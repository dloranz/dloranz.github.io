TMCC Astronomy Students Engaged in Novae Research

Thanks to an ongoing collaboration I have with U of Alaska astronomer Travis Rector, students in my AST 104 sections are participating in authentic research concerning novae in neighboring galaxies.

Using real data provided by Dr. Rector, students in my astronomy section are searching for novae in the Andromeda galaxy and measuring their light curves. Because this is newly acquired data, my students are truly discovering new novae &mdash seeing these novae before anyone else.

Most students in my AST 104 section really enjoy this part of the course, noting that they like the hands-on aspect of this project, and that it's really pretty cool to be the first to find a nova.
